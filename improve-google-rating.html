How to calculate the number of reviews to raise your Google rating

Improving your Google rating is a question of maths. The displayed rating is a weighted average of all your reviews. If you currently have 20 reviews with a 3.0 rating, you need around 40 five-star reviews to reach 4.3. With 50 existing reviews at 3.5, it will take about 50 new ones to reach 4.2. We work out the ideal pack for every situation.

Going from 3 to 4 stars: how many reviews do you need?

The jump from 3 to 4 stars is the most critical, because it is the threshold below which most customers ignore you. Depending on the number of existing reviews, expect between 20 and 80 five-star reviews to clear that bar. The investment pays for itself very quickly thanks to the influx of new customers that a rating above 4 generates.
